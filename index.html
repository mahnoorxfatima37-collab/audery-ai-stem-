About Ariana

Biography & career

Ariana Grande (full name Ariana Grande-Butera) is one of the most successful and influential pop stars of her generation. Born on June 26, 1993, in Boca Raton, Florida, she's currently 32 years old (as of February 2026). She's renowned for her four-octave vocal range (including whistle notes), signature high ponytail, and powerful blend of pop, R&B, and dance music.

She first gained fame as an actress on Nickelodeon shows like Victorious (2010–2013) as Cat Valentine and Sam & Cat (2013–2014), but transitioned fully to music with her debut album Yours Truly in 2013.

Music Career Highlights
Ariana has released seven studio albums (as of early 2026), with six reaching #1 on the Billboard 200. She's sold over 90 million records worldwide, earned three Grammy Awards, and holds numerous chart records— including many #1 hits on the Billboard Hot 100.

Her main studio albums include:
Yours Truly (2013) — Debut with hits like "The Way"
My Everything (2014) — Featuring "Problem," "Bang Bang," "Break Free"
Dangerous Woman (2016) — Title track and "Into You," "Side to Side"
Sweetener (2018) — "No Tears Left to Cry," "God is a Woman"
Thank U, Next (2019) — Massive hits like the title track, "7 Rings," "Break Up with Your Girlfriend"
Positions (2020) — "Positions," "34+35"
Eternal Sunshine (2024) — Lead singles "yes, and?," "we can't be friends (wait for your love)," "the boy is mine"; deluxe versions like Brighter Days Ahead followed in 2025.

She's also contributed to soundtracks, notably starring as Glinda in the 2024/2025 Wicked films (with Wicked: For Good in late 2025), earning award nominations and performing songs like "Popular" and "Defying Gravity."

As of February 2026, she has 98 entries on the Billboard Hot 100 (fourth-most for women) and ranks highly in streaming (around 85 million monthly Spotify listeners).

Recent Updates (as of February 2026)
She's preparing for The Eternal Sunshine Tour, her first major tour in six years, kicking off in June 2026 in North America (starting in Oakland, CA, with dates in LA, etc.) and including a London residency. It's described as a more limited run, with some reports calling it a potential "last hurrah" for large-scale touring as she focuses on acting and personal life.
